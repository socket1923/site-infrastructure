My lab

Socket23 is where I learn by building.

I use the lab to understand infrastructure, monitoring, security boundaries, recovery, local AI, and agent systems by operating them myself.

It gives me room to test ideas, observe failure modes, rebuild things, measure behavior, and turn the useful parts into projects and notes.

Current build

A persistent Network Assistant

I want an assistant that can tell me what the environment is supposed to look like, what it looks like now, what changed, what evidence supports a diagnosis, and whether the system actually recovered.

I’m building it around read-only infrastructure access, durable observations, source-backed knowledge, telemetry correlation, and human-controlled fixes.

The boundary I care about

  • Read-only access to production systems
  • Write access only to its own observation and knowledge stores
  • No production credentials that can make changes
  • Evidence and confidence attached to recommendations
  • Independent alerts when the AI layer fails

How I use the lab

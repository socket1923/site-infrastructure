About

Joseph Miller

Systems engineer and technical consultant

I work on infrastructure problems that cross the usual boundaries between servers, identity, storage, networking, cloud platforms, security controls, backup systems, and business applications.

My background

My role is often to enter an environment that is partially documented, aging, unstable, or difficult to change; determine how it actually works; identify the operational risks; and build a practical plan to stabilize or modernize it.

My experience spans Microsoft cloud and identity, Windows Server, Active Directory, VMware, Hyper-V, Proxmox, enterprise storage, networking, firewalls, backup and recovery, security monitoring, and local AI infrastructure.

I am comfortable moving between architecture and implementation. That may mean mapping dependencies for a migration, tracing an authentication failure through several systems, reviewing a firewall and DNS path, working from PowerShell or a Linux shell, validating a restore, or explaining the risk and sequence of a change to a non-specialist stakeholder.

I have also worked with network telemetry, SIEM platforms, honeypot data, and threat-analysis workflows. More recently, I have been applying the same infrastructure and security discipline to private AI systems, local inference, model-serving APIs, agent platforms, and bounded operational automation.

Working principles

Deliberate where risk is real

Verify before changing

I do not assume the environment matches the diagram, ticket, or original design. I inspect the system and confirm recovery options before a high-risk change.

Treat continuity as a requirement

A migration is not successful if the platform changes but the business cannot operate.

Follow the full failure path

An application problem may begin with DNS, identity, storage, networking, a certificate, a token, or a virtualization dependency.

Leave a usable record

I document findings, decisions, residual risk, validation, and the next safe action.

About me

I’m Joseph Miller. I like the problems without an obvious owner.

I’m at my best when a technical problem crosses boundaries: identity touches networking, storage changes affect virtualization, a cloud symptom starts on a Windows host, or a migration is really a recovery problem in disguise.

I live in Sandy, Oregon, and I spend a lot of my time building, troubleshooting, documenting, and learning how complex systems behave under real constraints.

How I got here

I did not follow one narrow product track. I kept following the problems. That took me through Windows Server, Active Directory, Microsoft 365, virtualization, SAN storage, networks, firewalls, backups, security monitoring, Linux, containers, automation, GPUs, local inference, and agent systems.

That breadth became one of my strengths. I can move between the architectural view and the low-level evidence: the migration sequence and the disk layout, the identity design and the token failure, the public service and the DNS, firewall, TLS, proxy, container, and application path behind it.

I care about safe execution because the most interesting systems are usually the ones people already depend on. I want a clear starting state, a recovery option, observable checkpoints, and proof that the complete function recovered—not just a green status beside the component I touched.

How I work

Curious, careful, and evidence-driven

I verify the environment

I do not assume the system matches the diagram, ticket, memory, or original design.

I make risk visible

I identify the authoritative data, hidden dependencies, irreversible steps, and recovery options before changing the system.

I write down what matters

I document the observed state, the decision, the result, what remains uncertain, and the next safe action.
